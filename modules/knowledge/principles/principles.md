## Principle 001 — Niche Depth Beats Audience Scale
**Source:** Alex Hormozi — LinkedIn post, June 2026
**Link:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/alexhormozi_she-has-5000-followers-her-average-post-share-7457256716720824320-eEAW/

### The Raw Insight
> "She has 5,000 followers. Her average post gets 20 likes. Takes home $1,000,000 a year."

A real business — registered dietitians billing insurance correctly. 5,800 Instagram followers.
19 likes per post. 1 comment. $1M/year revenue.

She doesn't need 5M followers. She doesn't need to hack the algorithm.
She understands one rule: **there is a real person on the other side of those views.**
Some businesses don't need more followers. They need the **right** followers.

### The Numbers That Matter
| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Followers | 5,800 |
| Avg. post likes | 19 |
| Avg. post comments | 1 |
| Annual revenue | $1,000,000 |
| Key | None of those follower/like numbers would predict $1M |

### Eva Operational Translation

**1. Deal Scoring — Competitor Analysis & Moat**
When evaluating a target business, audience SIZE is not a moat signal.
Audience SPECIFICITY is. A business with 500 deeply engaged niche subscribers
can outperform one with 50K broad followers. Eva should:
- Penalize businesses chasing vanity metrics (follower count, total impressions)
  without corresponding conversion rates or revenue per follower
- Reward businesses where the ICP is hyper-defined and the product is non-obvious
  to outsiders (dietitian billing ≠ obvious market — that's a moat)
- Flag "audience misfit" as a risk: if the content/community doesn't match
  the buyer persona, acquisition = marketing cost, not marketing asset

**2. Deal Scoring — Value Add**
When a neglected business has the RIGHT audience (even a small one),
value_add_score should weight re-engagement of existing audience
MORE than new audience growth. The asset is already there.
Re-engagement is cheaper and faster than acquisition.

**3. Content & GTM (Eva's own marketing)**
Eva does NOT need mass reach. Eva needs the right 500 founders to know
what it does. Every piece of content Eva produces should be written for
ONE specific person: the founder-operator who is about to make a bad
acquisition decision, or who is grinding without a system.
Volume is a vanity metric. Keyword conversions are the signal.

**4. batch.ai Application**
batch.ai's market is professional photographers billing at scale —
not casual Lightroom users. The right metric is not total Instagram
followers of the account, but: how many working photographers
(wedding, commercial, real estate) know this tool exists?
5,000 right photographers > 50,000 casual hobbyists.
Re-activating the email list to working pros = the $1M move here.

**5. Eva's Deal-Scout Rule (encoded)**
> "Don't ask how big the audience is. Ask how specific the problem is.
> The more specific the pain, the smaller the market needs to be
> to produce outsized revenue."
