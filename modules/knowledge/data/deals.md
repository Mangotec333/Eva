## Acquisition Thesis

**Category:** Health, wellness, and longevity SaaS.

**Why this category:**
- Recession-resistant (people don't stop managing their health in downturns)
- Regulatory moat (compliance complexity creates switching friction)
- AI-augmentable (most incumbents are pre-AI — digital engineering creates genuine improvement)
- Mission alignment (longevity economy is a personal thesis, not just a financial one)
- RCFE synergy (healthcare real estate + healthcare SaaS = compounding operator knowledge)

**What we're NOT buying:**
- E-commerce or physical product businesses (inventory, logistics, supplier risk)
- B2C subscription businesses with churn above 5%/month
- Businesses requiring the founder to be the product (personal brand, consulting)
- Anything with a dominant channel that can be de-platformed (TikTok-dependent, single SEO keyword)
- Businesses below $5K net/month (not enough cashflow to justify HELOC interest + operator time)

## Scoring Framework

Every deal is scored on five dimensions before advancing to due diligence.

| Dimension | Weight | What we measure |
|---|---|---|
| **AI-Proof Score** | 25% | Can AI commoditise this product's core value prop in 24 months? Lower = more at risk. 70+ = safe. |
| **Net Profit/Month** | 30% | Actual net after all expenses. Target: $10K+/month. Minimum: $8K/month. |
| **Operator Dependency** | 20% | How much of the business runs without the current owner? Target: 80%+ autonomous. |
| **Multiple (ask vs. market)** | 15% | Asking price ÷ monthly net. Market rate: 30x–40x. Target: under 30x. Red flag: over 45x. |
| **Category Fit** | 10% | Health / wellness / longevity alignment. Secondary: B2B SaaS, recurring revenue. |

**Minimum composite to advance to DD:** 65/100
**Target composite for LOI:** 75+/100

## Pattern Learnings

**Learning 1: Days on market is a seller motivation proxy.**
Listings that have been on Empire Flippers or Flippa for 45+ days have motivated sellers. Use that. Lead with a clean LOI at a 20% discount to asking. Most sellers in this range have never received a clean, well-structured offer — they respond to professionalism.

**Learning 2: Operator dependency is the most consistently underweighted risk.**
Every deal looks clean on revenue. The P&L rarely shows how much of that revenue is founder-delivered. The right question: "If the current owner takes a 6-week vacation with zero internet access, what percentage of revenue survives?" If the answer is below 70%, it's a job, not a business.

**Learning 3: The AI-proof score changes the category conversation.**
Healthcare compliance, senior living, and HIPAA-adjacent SaaS score consistently higher on AI-proof because the liability is human and the regulatory moat is real. This validates the health/wellness/longevity thesis from a durability standpoint, not just a market size standpoint.

**Learning 4: Direct outreach deals have better multiples.**
Deals sourced off-platform (direct outreach, operator referrals) come in at 20–25x multiples vs. 30–40x on Empire Flippers. The tradeoff: more DD work upfront (no broker vetting), more relationship management. Worth it if the multiple compression is 10x+.

**Learning 5: The first LOI sets the negotiation anchor.**
Don't let the seller's asking price be the anchor. Submit a clean LOI at your target multiple, with rationale. The worst outcome is a counter. The upside is an accepted offer at a number that pencils cleanly.

## Kill Criteria for a Deal

- Revenue declining MoM for 3+ consecutive months with no clear recovery plan from seller.
- Operator dependency score > 30% (more than 30% of revenue at risk if founder exits).
- AI-proof score < 60 (core value prop can be commoditised by a $20/month AI tool in 24 months).
- Multiple > 40x monthly net with no growth catalyst or proprietary asset justifying premium.
- Traffic source > 60% concentrated in one channel (SEO, paid, or single social platform).
- Seller evasive, slow, or inconsistent on P&L, traffic analytics, or churn data.
- Legal or compliance risk that cannot be quantified and capped in the purchase agreement.
- Post-acquisition integration requires full-time Vineet involvement for > 90 days.
