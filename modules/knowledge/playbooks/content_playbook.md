# Content Playbook
_Last updated: 2026-05-19 | Version: 0.1 | Module: EVA Knowledge OS_

_LinkedIn content framework for founder-operators who are also building a product. Voice, format, mechanics, and platform rules. Written for Vineet's context — adapt the thesis, keep the framework._

---

## Brand Voice

Three voices. One person. Know which one is speaking.

### Voice 1: The Operator
**When to use:** Deal flow posts, acquisition updates, RCFE learnings, capital allocation decisions.
**Tone:** Precise, numerical, no-nonsense. States things as facts, not opinions.
**Example opening:** "Deployed $200K HELOC. Target: $10K net/month after debt service. Here's the scoring framework I use."
**Signals:** Numbers in the first line. Concrete outcomes. No hedging.

### Voice 2: The Builder
**When to use:** EVA module launches, technical learnings, architecture decisions, AI tools.
**Tone:** Excited but grounded. Shows the work. Acknowledges what broke.
**Example opening:** "Built a FastAPI knowledge module last night. It stores my operating principles as queryable JSON. Here's why that matters for solo founders."
**Signals:** Behind-the-scenes content. Process over polish. "Here's what I learned" framing.

### Voice 3: The Thinker
**When to use:** Big idea posts, longevity economy thesis, philosophy of work, meditation/Ayurveda intersections, Collins/Hormozi frameworks applied personally.
**Tone:** Reflective, expansive, vulnerable without being soft.
**Example opening:** "Most founders exit and take everything with them — the pattern recognition, the instincts, the operating principles. That's a tragedy."
**Signals:** Starts with an observation, ends with a framework. Longer posts. Earns the scroll.

**Rule:** Every post is anchored in one voice. Mixed voice posts confuse the algorithm and the reader. Choose before writing.

## Post Format

Every post follows this structure — in order.

### 1. Hook (1–2 lines)
The only job of the hook is to stop the scroll. It must create:
- **Curiosity** ("Most founders get this backwards")
- **Specificity** ("$200K deployed. 48-hour experiment. Here's what happened.")
- **Pain recognition** ("If you've manually tracked deal flow in a spreadsheet, read this.")
- **Audacity** ("I tagged Alex Hormozi in a cold LinkedIn post. Here's why.")

No "In today's post I'll be sharing..." — that's the hook that gets scrolled.

### 2. Story or Context (3–6 lines)
The setup. What problem, what situation, what observation led to the value below. Be specific. Real details. A real number is worth 10 vague descriptions.

### 3. Value (5–10 lines)
The actual content. Framework, lesson, data, decision logic. This is what earns the share. Use short paragraphs (1–2 lines max). White space is readability. Lists work when the items are genuinely parallel. Avoid bullet soup.

### 4. CTA (1–2 lines)
One clear ask. Not three asks. Options:
- **Keyword CTA:** "Comment SCOUT and I'll send you early access." (generates algorithm signal + warm leads)
- **Poll CTA:** "Which of these is your biggest pain? Vote below." (engagement without friction)
- **Save CTA:** "Save this post — you'll want it when you're evaluating your next deal."
- **Follow CTA:** Only when the post has outperformed and the follow is earned, not begged.

### 5. Hashtags (3 max)
Three hashtags. Niche over broad. Examples: #SaaSAcquisition #FounderOS #LongevityEconomy
Never: #entrepreneur #business #success (too broad to surface to relevant audience)

**Format rules:**
- Max 1,300 characters for full visibility without "see more" click (unless the Thinker voice earns the scroll)
- First line stands alone (it's what shows in the feed)
- No external links in the post body (LinkedIn suppresses reach). Put links in comments.
- Line breaks after every 1–2 sentences. Dense paragraphs don't get read.

## Audience Participation Mechanics

### Keyword CTAs
**How it works:** CTA asks the audience to comment a specific word. EVA (or manual process) DMs every commenter within 60 minutes with the next step.

**Why it works:**
- Every comment expands algorithmic reach (LinkedIn's engagement signal)
- The keyword filters for high-intent users (they read far enough to see the CTA)
- Creates a warm lead list before a product exists (sell first principle in action)
- The DM conversation is personal — converts better than a landing page

**Keyword assignment:**
- SCOUT → Deal Scout / acquisition entrepreneur track
- OPERATOR → RCFE Compliance AI / healthcare operator track
- EVA → EVA OS waitlist / founder-operator track
- BUILD → AI Growth Agency inquiry

**Rule:** One keyword per post. Multiple keywords dilute the signal.

### Polls
**Best for:** Validating assumptions before building. "Which of these is your biggest pain?" Polls generate passive engagement from people who won't comment. Useful for audience research, less useful for lead generation. Use polls in the Thinker and Operator voices. Don't use polls to replace keyword CTAs — they don't generate warm leads.

### Comment Amplification
**The first hour matters most.** LinkedIn's algorithm determines reach in the first 60–90 minutes based on engagement velocity. Strategy:
- Post at peak time (7–9am or 12–1pm on weekdays for founder-operator audience)
- Reply to every comment within the first hour — every reply re-notifies the commenter and creates a second engagement signal
- Ask a follow-up question in each reply to keep the thread alive
- Do not post and disappear. The first hour requires presence.

## Platform Adaptation Rules

### LinkedIn (Primary)
- Audience: founder-operators, acquisition entrepreneurs, RCFE operators, healthcare tech buyers
- Format: short paragraphs, no external links, keyword CTAs
- Posting frequency: 3–5x/week during sprint. Quality > frequency.
- Best content: Operator voice (deals, capital, frameworks) + Thinker voice (big ideas, longevity thesis)
- What doesn't work: highly technical content (wrong audience), personal life content (off-brand), vague inspiration

### Reddit (Secondary — cold audience test)
- Audience: r/entrepreneurship, r/SaaS, r/acquisitions — larger volume, lower intent
- Format: genuine post, no hard sells. "Here's what I learned doing X" performs. Pitch posts get removed.
- Rule: Provide 10x value before any CTA. Reddit has a finely tuned bullshit detector.
- Best content: Detailed breakdowns, honest failure stories, AMA-style posts
- What doesn't work: Promotional framing, product links in first post, anything that feels like marketing

### Twitter/X (Tertiary — amplification)
- Use to amplify LinkedIn posts that hit. Short form summary + link to LinkedIn post.
- Threads work for Builder voice (step-by-step technical walkthroughs)
- Not a primary channel. Feed it with repurposed LinkedIn content.

## The 48-Hour Engagement Window

**Why the first 48 hours define a post:**
LinkedIn's algorithm uses early engagement velocity to decide whether to expand or suppress a post's reach. A post with 20 comments in hour 1 gets shown to 10x the audience of a post with 2 comments.

**The 48-hour protocol:**
- Hour 0–1: Post + reply to every comment immediately
- Hour 1–6: Monitor keyword comments + trigger DMs within 60 minutes of each comment
- Hour 6–24: Post a "top comment" reply to keep the thread surfaced
- Hour 24–48: Assess engagement vs. kill/fuel signals. If fuel signal hit → launch $200 ad spend on the post.
- After 48 hours: Move to next post. Don't flog a dead post.

## Kill or Fuel Signals for Content Strategy

**Kill a content format when:**
- 3+ consecutive posts in the same format get under 500 impressions or 0 keyword comments
- Hook style generates scrolls but no engagement (high impressions, low comments ratio)
- Audience persona is wrong — engagement comes from outside the target segments

**Fuel a content format when:**
- 3+ keyword comments within 48 hours on any single post
- 5+ inbound DMs from target persona after a post
- A post from a specific voice (Operator, Builder, Thinker) significantly outperforms the others — that voice is where the audience is, double down

**Quarterly reset:**
- Review top 10 performing posts. What voice? What format? What topic?
- Review bottom 10. What patterns caused underperformance?
- Adjust the template based on data, not gut.
