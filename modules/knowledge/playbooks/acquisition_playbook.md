# Acquisition Playbook
_Last updated: 2026-05-19 | Version: 0.1 | Module: EVA Knowledge OS_

_Reusable framework for solo operators acquiring online businesses in the $100K–$500K range. Written for founders with a HELOC or similar acquisition capital, no institutional backing, and a bias toward health/wellness/longevity._

---

## The Thesis

**Why health, wellness, and longevity SaaS?**

1. **Recession-resistant demand.** Healthcare spending doesn't track economic cycles the way consumer goods do. People manage chronic conditions, mental health, and aging regardless of the macro environment.

2. **Regulatory moat.** HIPAA, state compliance, licensing requirements — these create switching friction that keeps customers sticky even when alternatives appear. The compliance complexity that scares off most buyers is a feature for operators who understand it.

3. **AI-augmentable, not AI-replaceable.** Most incumbents in this space were built pre-2020. The interface is dated. The automation is minimal. A digital engineering moat — even a modest one — creates genuine product differentiation without requiring venture scale.

4. **Mission alignment unlocks operator energy.** The longevity economy is not just a market thesis. When the work connects to a personal conviction (quality of care, human longevity, aging well), the operator energy doesn't need to be manufactured. That matters for a solo operator running lean.

5. **RCFE synergy.** A healthcare real estate operator who also owns healthcare SaaS has a distribution advantage, a credibility advantage, and a data advantage. The two businesses teach each other.

**What we pass on:**
- B2C with >5% monthly churn
- E-commerce / physical inventory
- Personal brand businesses (founder = product)
- Single-channel traffic dependencies (one SEO keyword, one paid platform)
- Any deal below $5K net/month (doesn't cover HELOC interest + operator time)

## Capital Structure

**Vehicle:** HELOC (Home Equity Line of Credit)
- Total: $200K @ 9.5%
- Monthly carrying cost: ~$1,583/month (interest-only)
- Break-even net/month needed to cover cost of capital: $2K (with buffer)
- Target net/month post-acquisition: $10K+ (6.3x cost of capital)

**Why HELOC over SBA or acquisition loan:**
- Speed: HELOC closes in days. SBA takes months.
- Flexibility: draw as needed, repay and redraw. Better for staged capital deployment.
- Control: no lender approval on the specific acquisition. The asset is already underwritten (your home).

**Capital allocation sequence:**
1. Acquisition purchase price (first dollar deployed)
2. Working capital buffer (2–3 months operating expenses)
3. Integration costs (tech migration, contractor onboarding, light marketing)
4. EVA deployment (automate operations within 90 days)

**Exit target:** 2.5x–3x return. If acquired at 30x monthly net, exit at 60x–80x monthly net after 18–24 months of growth. Redeploy into next acquisition.

## Sourcing

**Tier 1 — Broker marketplaces (highest volume, highest multiples):**
- Empire Flippers: best for vetted SaaS $100K+. Pre-screened revenue. Multiples run 30–45x.
- Flippa: broader range, more noise, better for finding motivated sellers at 20–25x. Requires more filtering.
- MicroAcquire (Acquire.com): early-stage SaaS, often pre-revenue or very low MRR. Pass unless growth rate is exceptional.

**Tier 2 — Direct outreach (lowest multiples, highest DD burden):**
- Deal Scout v2 automated pipeline: searches for health/wellness SaaS with 3+ years operating, consistent revenue, low growth (seller motivation signal), no recent sale attempt.
- Cold email to founders of businesses that fit the profile: "I'm a solo operator looking to acquire [category]. I pay cash, close fast, and want a clean transition."
- Operator referral networks: RCFE contacts, LinkedIn connections in health tech.

**Tier 3 — Inbound (passive, improves over time):**
- LinkedIn presence ("I acquire health/wellness SaaS") generates inbound from sellers who prefer discretion over brokers.
- EVA content engine posts on acquisition thesis → sellers DM.

**Sourcing rule:** Always have 5–10 deals in the pipeline simultaneously. Never negotiate from a position of having one option.

## Scoring

Use the five-dimension scoring framework from deals.md. Minimum 65/100 to advance to due diligence. Target 75+ for LOI.

**Quick filter (before scoring):**
1. Net profit/month ≥ $8K? → Pass. Otherwise skip.
2. Operator dependency: can the business run without the founder for 6 weeks? → If no, skip.
3. Revenue source: is more than 60% from one channel or one customer? → If yes, skip.
4. Is the core value prop at risk from a $20/month AI tool in 24 months? → AI-proof score < 60? Skip.
5. Asking multiple ≤ 40x? → If above 40x with no growth catalyst, skip.

Pass all 5 filters → score. Score 65+ → request data room.

## Due Diligence Checklist

Complete all 10 items before submitting LOI.

1. **P&L — 24 months.** Month-by-month revenue, expenses, net. Look for seasonality, step-downs, one-time revenue. Calculate trailing 6-month and trailing 12-month averages separately.

2. **Traffic analytics — 12 months.** Google Analytics or equivalent. Source breakdown (organic, paid, direct, referral). Any single source >60% is concentration risk.

3. **Churn data.** Monthly churn rate by cohort. For SaaS: acceptable <3%/month. >5%/month is a red flag. Ask for the reason behind top 5 churn reasons.

4. **Customer concentration.** What % of revenue is from the top 10 customers? >50% from one customer is a blocker. >30% from top 3 is a flag.

5. **Tech stack audit.** What does the product run on? Who maintains it? Is there a single engineer with tribal knowledge? Can it be migrated to EVA-managed infrastructure within 90 days?

6. **Contracts and obligations.** Any revenue-sharing agreements, exclusivity clauses, or contracts with >6-month remaining terms that transfer with the business?

7. **IP and assets.** Domain, brand, codebase, content library, email list — what exactly transfers? Get list in writing.

8. **Seller transition terms.** Will seller stay for 60–90 day handoff? What's their availability? What tribal knowledge isn't in the docs?

9. **Legal and compliance.** Any outstanding legal disputes, HIPAA compliance status (if applicable), state licensing requirements. Get a legal opinion if compliance risk is material.

10. **Growth hypothesis.** Why has the business not grown faster? What's the one thing the current owner would do with 3x the resources? This conversation reveals both opportunity and hidden problems.

## Integration: First 90 Days Post-Acquisition

**Days 1–30: Stabilise**
- Don't change anything. Document everything.
- Onboard to all tools, logins, and communication channels.
- Meet top 5 customers personally (call or email). Introduce yourself. Ask one question: "What would make this product indispensable to you?"
- Identify the single highest-risk operational dependency and build a backup.

**Days 31–60: Systematise**
- Deploy EVA Logger and Morning OS for the acquired business.
- Automate the top 3 recurring manual tasks (onboarding, billing, reporting).
- Migrate tech stack to EVA-managed infrastructure if applicable.
- Set up weekly KPI dashboard: MRR, churn, tickets, NPS proxy.

**Days 61–90: Improve**
- Ship one product improvement sourced directly from customer feedback (Days 1–30).
- Launch one growth experiment (SEO, outreach, referral program).
- Document the operating playbook: who does what, when, how. This is the asset you'll use to exit at a premium.
- Baseline financial review: actual net vs. acquisition model. Adjust or flag variance.

## Exit Criteria

**When to sell:**
- MRR growth rate has plateaued for 6+ consecutive months and no growth lever is visible within 90 days.
- An offer comes in at 50x+ monthly net and you can redeploy into a larger deal.
- A strategic acquirer offers 60x+ and wants the EVA integration as part of the acquisition thesis.
- The business requires >10 hours/week of founder involvement and the opportunity cost exceeds the cashflow.

**How to exit at a premium:**
- Clean, documented operating playbook (the buyer pays for certainty)
- EVA integration as a product feature (increases AI-proof score for next buyer)
- Positive MoM growth in the trailing 6 months (multiples expand with growth)
- Willing to offer 60-day post-sale support (reduces buyer risk perception)

**Target exit multiple:** 50x–80x monthly net (vs. 25x–35x acquisition). The delta is the value created by EVA integration, systematisation, and growth.
