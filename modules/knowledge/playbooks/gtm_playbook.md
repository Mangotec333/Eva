# GTM Playbook
_Last updated: 2026-05-19 | Version: 0.1 | Module: EVA Knowledge OS_

_Go-to-market framework for micro-SaaS products built by solo founder-operators. Designed for speed, low capital, and high learning velocity. The principle is simple: sell before you build, validate before you spend._

---

## Sell First Principle

**Never build a product that hasn't been sold first.**

"Sold first" means at least one of the following:
- A human typed a keyword in response to a CTA post and then engaged with a DM
- Someone clicked "join waitlist" or filled out a Typeform with their email
- Someone paid $1 (even $1 means intent — it is categorically different from "I'd use that")
- A founder said "I would pay $X/month for this" in a recorded conversation

Building without this is guessing. Guessing costs months. Testing costs 48 hours.

**The sequence:**
1. Write the pain point in one sentence. ("RCFE operators manually track compliance across 47 state-mandated checkpoints. It takes 6 hours/week and they still miss things.")
2. Write the value prop in one sentence. ("Compliance AI tracks all 47 checkpoints automatically and alerts you 14 days before each deadline.")
3. Post it. Include a keyword CTA.
4. Count keyword comments at 48 hours.
5. If ≥3 → build the landing page. If 0 → rewrite the pain point and repeat.
6. If landing page converts ≥5% → build the MVP.

**The inversion:** most founders build, then try to find customers. This playbook inverts that. The customer finds the product before the product exists. The product gets built because the customer validated the need.

## Channel Sequencing

### Phase 1: Borrow (Months 1–3)
**Goal:** Get in front of existing audiences without owning them.

- LinkedIn organic content (target persona: founder-operators, acquisition entrepreneurs, RCFE operators)
- Reddit posts in relevant subreddits (r/SaaS, r/entrepreneurship, r/smallbusiness)
- Twitter/X threads repurposing LinkedIn hits
- Partner content: guest post, podcast appearance, co-post with aligned operator

**Rules in Phase 1:**
- Zero ad spend until organic validation is confirmed
- Track every piece of content: impressions, comments, keywords, DMs
- Every channel gets 5 posts before judging performance (insufficient sample otherwise)

### Phase 2: Capture (Months 2–4)
**Goal:** Convert borrowed audience into owned email list and waitlist.

- Keyword CTA DM sequences → Typeform → email list
- Early access page (Notion or simple HTML) with email capture
- "First 20 get roadmap input + lifetime discount" mechanics
- Weekly email digest to list (2–3 sentences, one link, one insight)

**Rules in Phase 2:**
- Email list > followers. An email list you own. A LinkedIn following can be de-platformed.
- Do not sell to the list until you have ≥100 subscribers and have delivered 4 weeks of value with no ask.
- Segment the list by persona immediately (SCOUT, OPERATOR, EVA, BUILD keywords map to segments).

### Phase 3: Monetise (Months 3–6)
**Goal:** Convert validated audience into paying customers.

- Launch freemium tier to existing list first (not public launch)
- Offer early adopter pricing (30% discount, locked in for life)
- First 20 paid customers get 1:1 onboarding call + roadmap input
- Move to public launch only after first 20 paying customers are live and talking

### Phase 4: Own (Month 4+)
**Goal:** Build owned community that generates ongoing distribution.

- LinkedIn group or Circle community for the target persona
- Weekly AMA or office hours (20 min, free for all, upsell in DMs)
- Community-generated content (ask members to share their wins/frameworks)
- This is where the moat transitions from borrowed → owned

## Pricing Ladder

**Framework:** Start free to get volume. Gate the features that require the product to earn its keep. Charge for outcomes, not features.

| Tier | Price | What's included | Conversion goal |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Free** | $0 | Core feature, limited usage (5 deals/week, 2 posts/week) | 100+ users to prove retention |
| **Starter** | $49/month | Unlimited core feature + email alerts | 10% of free users who are active weekly |
| **Pro** | $99/month | + AI scoring, daily brief, priority support | Power users who use the product daily |
| **Operator** | $299/month | Full EVA OS: all modules, Angels, Morning Brief, community | Teams and operators running the product for clients |

**Pricing rules:**
- Never start with a paid-only model. The free tier generates word-of-mouth and social proof.
- The free tier must be genuinely useful — not crippled. If it's crippled, users churn before they see value.
- The jump from Free to Starter must be obvious in value. If the user can't immediately see what $49/month buys, the gate is in the wrong place.
- Annual pricing: offer 20% discount for annual commitment. Improves cashflow, improves LTV, reduces churn.

**When to raise prices:**
- When 20%+ of new users choose the top tier without hesitation
- When churn drops below 3%/month (people are staying — you've earned the right to charge more)
- When inbound requests cite the product by name (brand has formed)

## Early Adopter Mechanics

**The first 20 customers are your co-builders, not your beta testers.**

Early adopter offer:
- Lifetime price lock (pay $49/month forever, even if list price goes to $99)
- Direct access to the roadmap (monthly 30-min call with Vineet or async Loom)
- First access to new modules before public launch
- Case study opportunity (you become the reference customer)

**How to recruit early adopters:**
- Post about the product and use keyword CTA ("Comment BUILD if you want early access")
- DM every keyword commenter personally within 60 minutes with a 2-sentence pitch and a Typeform link
- Typeform asks: pain point, current solution, willingness to pay, contact info
- Follow up with the top 20 responders within 24 hours with early adopter offer

**What to do with early adopters:**
- Weekly check-in (5-minute async Loom: "Here's what shipped this week, what broke, what's next")
- Ask for one piece of feedback per week: "What's the one thing that would make this 10x more useful to you?"
- Ask for a testimonial at Day 30 (before they've had time to forget how painful life was before)
- Ask for a referral at Day 60 (if they're still using it, they know someone else who needs it)

## Freemium Gate Design

The gate is a product design decision, not a pricing decision.

**Principles:**
- Gate volume, not quality. The free tier should do the core job well — just less often. Gating the good features makes the free tier useless and generates churn.
- The gate should create a moment where the user thinks: "I hit the limit exactly when I was getting value. I need more."
- Common effective gates: number of uses per week/month, team collaboration, integrations, API access, priority processing, export formats.
- Bad gates: core features behind paywall before value is experienced, setup/onboarding behind paywall, support gated too aggressively on free tier.

**EVA freemium gate (current design):**
- Free: 5 deal alerts/week + 2 LinkedIn post drafts/week (hit the limit right as you're building the habit)
- Starter ($49): unlimited alerts + posts + email digest
- Pro ($99): + AI scoring engine + Morning Brief + 1 Angel
- Operator ($299): full OS + all Angels + Knowledge OS + community access

**Gate review cadence:** Review the gate design every 30 days. If free-to-paid conversion is <5% of active users, the gate is either in the wrong place or the free tier isn't generating enough habit.

## When to Spend on Ads

**Rule: Ads accelerate what already works. They do not fix what doesn't.**

Organic signal is required before any paid spend. If a post doesn't generate keyword comments organically, running ads on it will generate impressions, not leads.

**The ad trigger:**
- A post gets 3+ keyword comments in 48 hours organically → $200 LinkedIn ad spend on that exact post
- Target audience: same persona as the organic commenters (use LinkedIn matched audiences or job title targeting)
- Duration: 7 days. Measure: additional keyword comments + DM conversion rate
- If CPC is under $5 and keyword conversion rate is over 10% → increase budget to $500
- If CPC is over $15 with <5% conversion → kill the ad, rewrite the hook

**Ad spend budget caps:**
- Month 1: $200/test, max 2 tests ($400 total)
- Month 2: $200/test, max 4 tests if Month 1 had positive ROI ($800 total)
- Month 3+: scale based on cost-per-keyword-lead vs. LTV of converted users

**Never spend on ads before:**
- 5 organic posts with measurable engagement data
- At least 1 post with a confirmed keyword comment
- A landing page or Typeform capturing leads from ad click
- A defined conversion event (keyword, email, paid signup)
